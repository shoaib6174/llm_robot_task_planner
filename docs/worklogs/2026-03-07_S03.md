### Built Two-Room World (`worlds/two_room_world.sdf`)
- Layout: two 4m x 4m rooms, connected by 1m doorway at center of dividing wall
- 6 walls: 4 outer (0.15m thick, 2.5m tall) + 2 divider segments with doorway gap
- Floor panels: light beige (Room A), light gray-blue (Room B) for visual distinction
- 3 lights: directional sun + 2 point lights (one per room ceiling)
- 4 colored cubes (6cm, 0.1kg): red (2.5, 2.5), blue (1.0, 1.0) in Room A; green (6.0, 2.5), yellow (6.5, 1.0) in Room B
- Cube inertia: I = (1/6)*m*s^2 = 0.00006 for each axis
- Friction mu=1.0 on cubes for reliable grasping later
- Plugins: Physics, UserCommands, SceneBroadcaster, Contact, Imu, Sensors (ogre2)
- GUI section with initial camera pose looking at both rooms

## Decisions Made
| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| Build world from scratch instead of adapting existing | No suitable two-room world found; simple box geometry is faster and more controllable |
| 4m x 4m rooms (vs 3m x 2.3m from PRD's ~75 sq ft) | Larger rooms give more navigation space for robot, prevents tight-space issues with Nav2 |
| 0.15m wall thickness | Thin enough to not waste space, thick enough for reliable collision detection |
| Floor panels per room (visual only, no collision) | Helps distinguish rooms in camera feed and demo video without affecting physics |
| SDF 1.8 format | Matches ros_gz_sim_demos reference; compatible with Gz Sim 8 |
